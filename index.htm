We use the word “accommodation.” Accommodations describes academic adjustments to provide equal access to the learning environment. Accommodations reduce barriers related to disabilitIies.  
Persons with disabilities are welcomed as equal members into the diverse and inclusive environment of the college. Accommodations are arranged and supported through your Disability Support Services (DSS) office.

Getting the Support You Need and Deserve

The 4 Steps

  1. INTAKE:
    1. Schedule an intake appointment with your campus DSS office. Intake appointments are usually one hour in length.
    2. Bring a photo ID, Student number, and documentation of disability.
      This information will help us provide the best support possible.
    3. If your documentation is not yet available, or is in question, come to the intake appointment anyway.
    4. Contact DSS Contact Disability Support Services (DSS) on your home campus. We look forward to meeting you!
  2. ACTIVATE: After completing an intake appointment, you receive notification of approved accommodations within 10 business days. You must then activate the accommodations by submitting the online request form.
    1. A Letter of Accommodation will be sent to you and your instructors via your FRCC email.
    2. Request early! If you request is received less than three (3) weeks prior to the start of the semester, the process may be delayed.
    3. Accommodations are not retroactive and do not begin until an official FRCC Letter of Accommodation has been delivered to you and your professors.
  3. RENEW: Every semester, as soon as you register for future classes, request your accommodations by completing the online form. FIND THE FORM for YOUR CAMPUS 
  4. COMMUNICATE: It is your responsibility to communicate with your instructors throughout the semester:
    1. To confirm your Instructors have received the accommodation letter, and
    2. To coordinate with them how your student accommodations will be fulfilled for their class(es).
    3. To cooperate with them throughout the semester so you both can understand and evaluate how your accommodations are working.
    4. Concerns? If you have any questions or concerns, contact your campus DSS office.
